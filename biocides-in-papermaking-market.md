<p><p>In the papermaking industry, biocides play a crucial role in controlling microbial growth and ensuring product quality. The market can be categorized into inorganic and organic biocides. Inorganic biocides, such as chlorine dioxide and zinc-based compounds, offer effective antimicrobial properties and are often used in water treatment processes. Organic biocides, including quaternary ammonium compounds and phenols, are favored for their lower environmental impact and effectiveness in preventing slime and biofilm formation during papermaking. Both types enhance performance and extend shelf life.</p></p>

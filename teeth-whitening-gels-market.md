<p><p>Teeth whitening gels primarily consist of two key types: carbamide peroxide and hydrogen peroxide. Carbamide peroxide breaks down into hydrogen peroxide when applied, offering gradual whitening effects and is often gentle on enamel. Hydrogen peroxide, a more potent agent, delivers faster results but can cause tooth sensitivity if used excessively. Both types are popular in home whitening kits and professional treatments, catering to varying consumer preferences for effectiveness, application time, and potential side effects.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Transmission Type</li><li>Reflectance Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Co-oximetry refers to the measurement of various hemoglobin species in the blood. The two primary market types are transmission and reflectance co-oximetry. Transmission co-oximetry involves passing light through a blood sample to quantify hemoglobin levels, while reflectance co-oximetry analyzes the light reflected off the sample. Each type has its applications in clinical settings, where accuracy and speed are crucial for patient care. The choice between them often depends on the specific requirements of the testing environment.</p></p>

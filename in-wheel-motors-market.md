<p><p>In-wheel motors are categorized into two main types: inner rotor and outer rotor. Inner rotor motors feature the rotor located inside the stator, providing compact design and efficient cooling, making them suitable for various vehicle applications. Conversely, outer rotor motors have the rotor positioned outside the stator, resulting in higher torque density and improved power delivery, which enhances vehicle performance. Both designs cater to the growing demand for electric vehicles, emphasizing efficiency and compactness in modern automotive engineering.</p></p>
